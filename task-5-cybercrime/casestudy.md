Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
---

What is crime
--
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into sending money or revealing sensitive banking details. It usually happens through fake calls, messages, or links that appear to be from trusted sources such as banks or payment apps.

How it happens
--
In a typical scenario, a fraudster contacts the victim pretending to be a bank representative or a buyer/seller. First, they gain the victim’s trust by sounding professional and urgent. Next, they ask the victim to click on a link or approve a “collect request” on their UPI app. The victim, unaware of the risk, enters their UPI PIN, believing they are receiving money or verifying their account. In reality, this action authorizes a payment to the fraudster. Within seconds, money is transferred from the victim’s account.

Targeted
--
Students, elderly people, and individuals unfamiliar with digital payments are the most common targets. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online systems. In some cases, recovering the money is difficult if not reported immediately.

Prevention
--
This type of fraud can be prevented by never sharing UPI PINs, avoiding unknown links, and verifying requests before approving any transaction. Awareness and cautious behavior are key to staying safe from such cybercrimes.
